Bachelor of Science in Information Systems

The B.S. in Information Systems program prepares students for all potential career positions in the IS field, including Systems Analysis, Design, Computer Networking, and Health Care Informatics.

Current Required Courses

  • Information Systems: IS 300, IS 310, IS 410, IS 420, IS 425, IS 436, IS 450, IS 451, Upper Level IS Elective
  • Mathematics and Computer Science: MATH 155, MATH 215, STAT 351, IS 147 or CMSC 201, IS 247 or CMSC 202, One additional approved programming course (e.g., IS246, IS413, IS448, IS471, IS472)
  • Administrative Science: MGMT 210, ECON 101, ECON 102, ECON 121, ECON 122, ENGL 393 or IS 369

Notes:

  • Students must earn a grade of “C” or better for any course to be applied to the Information Systems major.
  • Before taking IS 410 and IS 450, students must complete MATH 155 and IS 147 with a grade of "C" or higher and IS 300 and IS 310 with a grade of "B" or higher.
  • Examples of upper-level electives: IS 303, IS 304, IS 460, IS 461, IS 427, IS 428, IS 430, IS 438, IS 464, IS 471, IS 472, etc.
